Repair FAQs

Clear details regarding our emergency AC repair protocol.

Why is my AC blowing warm air suddenly?

This is extremely common and is usually caused by a frozen evaporator coil or a catastrophic refrigerant leak in the lines. If the system lacks freon, the compressor will run constantly without actually lowering the ambient temperature of your home. You should turn off the thermostat immediately and call us to prevent burning out the expensive compressor motor entirely.

How long does an emergency repair usually take?

The vast majority of our emergency service calls are completely resolved in under two hours from the moment our technician steps onto your property. We carry over 90% of the required replacement parts directly on our trucks so we don't have to leave to visit a supply house. Our goal is to diagnose, quote, and fix the mechanism in one lightning-fast visit.

Will you charge me extra if I call on a Sunday?

No, we firmly believe that you should not be financially punished just because your mechanical equipment broke down outside of traditional business hours. Our flat-rate pricing model guarantees that you will pay the exact same fair rate on a Sunday night as you would on a Tuesday morning. We are committed to transparency and providing absolute relief without the predatory upcharges.

Should I repair my old AC or just replace it?

This heavily depends on the age of your system and the cost of the impending repair. If your air conditioner is over ten years old and facing a multi-thousand dollar compressor replacement or freon leak, investing in a new, highly efficient system is significantly smarter. However, if it’s a simple blown capacitor or contactor, a rapid repair is highly cost-effective and will buy you years of continued cooling.
